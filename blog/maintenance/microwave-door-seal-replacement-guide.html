Replace Your Microwave Door Seal in 5 Simple Steps

A damaged microwave door seal allows heat and radiation to escape, reducing cooking efficiency and creating a safety hazard. Replacing the seal is a straightforward DIY repair that can save you hundreds of dollars.

Signs Your Door Seal Needs Replacement

  • Visible cracks or tears in the gasket
  • Heat escaping around the microwave door during operation
  • Door doesn't close properly or feels loose
  • Food splatters escaping from the sides of the door
  • The seal is hardened, cracked, or discolored

Step-by-Step Replacement Guide

  1. Unplug the microwave: Always disconnect power before any repair
  2. Remove the old gasket: Carefully pull out the old seal from its channel
  3. Clean the seal channel: Use a damp cloth to remove all debris and residue
  4. Install the new gasket: Press the new seal firmly into the channel, starting at the top and working around the door
  5. Test the seal: Close the door firmly and check that it seals completely

Tips for Success

When replacing your microwave door seal, remember these helpful tips:

  • Order the correct replacement seal for your microwave model
  • Warm the new gasket slightly to make it more flexible
  • Apply a light coat of food-grade silicone lubricant to the channel
  • Take your time installing to avoid twists or gaps in the seal
