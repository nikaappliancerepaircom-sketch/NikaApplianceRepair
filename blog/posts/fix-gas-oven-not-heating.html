Fix Gas Oven Not Heating: Toronto Repair Guide 2025

December 07, 2025 Expert Team

A gas oven that won't heat is more than inconvenient - it can also indicate safety issues that need immediate attention. This guide walks you through the diagnostic process, explains what's safe to check yourself, and helps you understand when professional repair is essential.

Why Your Gas Oven Won't Heat

The Igniter: Most Common Culprit

Gas oven igniters are responsible for 80% of no-heat complaints. Here's how they work and fail:

  • How it works: The igniter glows hot (1,800¬∞F+) which opens the safety valve and ignites gas
  • Weak igniter: Glows orange but can't reach temperature to open valve - takes 60+ seconds
  • Dead igniter: No glow at all when bake is selected
  • Cracked igniter: Visible damage from thermal stress breaks the circuit

Gas Safety Valve Issues

The safety valve only opens when the igniter proves it's hot enough to ignite gas safely.

  • Valve coil failure: Electromagnetic coils wear out, won't respond to igniter
  • Stuck valve: Internal mechanism jammed closed
  • Calibration drift: Valve requires higher amperage than igniter provides

Burner and Orifice Problems

  • Clogged burner ports: Food debris blocks gas distribution
  • Orifice blockage: Spider webs or debris in gas orifice (common after storage)
  • Burner misalignment: Flames don't reach igniter location

Temperature Sensor Failures

  • Open circuit: Sensor wire broken, oven won't heat past initial ignition
  • Resistance drift: Inaccurate readings cause over/under heating
  • Sensor position: Moved or bent sensor gives wrong temperature

Safe Homeowner Checks

Step 1: Verify Gas Supply

  • Check that the gas shutoff valve behind oven is parallel to pipe (open)
  • Verify stovetop burners ignite normally - if not, gas supply issue
  • Confirm gas meter is functioning and account is active

Step 2: Observe the Igniter

  1. Remove oven racks for visibility
  2. Locate igniter (usually back center of oven floor or on burner assembly)
  3. Turn oven to Bake 350¬∞F
  4. Watch igniter through oven window or door slightly ajar
  5. A healthy igniter glows bright orange and gas ignites within 30-60 seconds
  6. Weak igniter glows dim orange/yellow and takes 90+ seconds or fails

Step 3: Check Temperature Accuracy

  1. Place oven thermometer on center rack
  2. Set oven to 350¬∞F and wait 20 minutes
  3. Compare thermometer to set temperature
  4. Variance over 25¬∞F indicates sensor or calibration issue

Step 4: Inspect Burner Assembly

  • When oven is cool, remove bottom panel (usually 2-4 screws)
  • Visually inspect burner for debris in flame ports
  • Check that igniter shows no visible cracks
  • Look for any signs of rust or corrosion
üí° Time the Ignition: Use your phone timer to measure from bake selection to flame ignition. Under 90 seconds is normal. Over 90 seconds means weak igniter. No ignition after 2-3 minutes triggers safety shutoff.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my gas oven igniter glow but the oven doesn't heat?

The igniter must draw 3.2-3.6 amps to open the safety valve. A glowing but weak igniter doesn't pull enough current. The safety valve stays closed as a protection against unburned gas. Replacing the igniter resolves this in nearly all cases.

Is it safe to replace a gas oven igniter myself?

While the igniter itself is an electrical component, accessing it involves working around gas connections. In Ontario, any work that could affect gas flow must be done by TSSA-certified technicians. We recommend professional replacement for safety and warranty compliance.

My oven heats but doesn't reach temperature - is that the igniter too?

Probably not. If gas ignites but temperature is wrong, the issue is usually the temperature sensor (oven probe) or calibration. The sensor sends resistance readings to the control board to regulate heat. Sensor replacement typically resolves this.

How long do gas oven igniters last?

Quality igniters last 5-8 years with normal use. Self-cleaning cycles stress igniters due to extreme heat (800-900¬∞F). If you use self-clean frequently, igniter life may be shorter. Some homeowners see 10+ years with careful use.

Should I repair or replace my 15-year-old gas oven?

For a single component failure like an igniter ($175-$245), repair makes sense. If you're facing multiple repairs (igniter plus valve plus sensor), the combined cost might approach 50% of replacement cost. We provide honest assessments to help you decide.
