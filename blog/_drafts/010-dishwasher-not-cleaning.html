Why Is My Dishwasher Not Cleaning Dishes?

1. Clogged Spray Arms (40%)

Food particles and mineral deposits clog spray arm holes, reducing water pressure.

DIY Fix: Remove spray arms, clean holes with toothpick, rinse under hot water. Cost: $0, Time: 10-15 min.

2. Improper Loading (30%)

Overloading or blocking spray arms prevents water from reaching all dishes.

Fix: Load properly‚Äîplates facing center, bowls angled down, nothing blocking spray arms.

3. Wrong Detergent or Old Pods (15%)

Expired pods, wrong detergent type, or too little detergent reduces cleaning power.

Fix: Use fresh pods (check exp date), fill both cups if using powder, use rinse aid.

4. Low Water Temperature

Water below 120¬∞F doesn't dissolve detergent or remove grease effectively.

Fix: Set water heater to 120-140¬∞F, run hot water at sink before starting dishwasher.

5. Clogged Filter

Dirty filter recirculates food particles onto dishes.

Fix: Clean filter weekly‚Äîremove, rinse under hot water, scrub with soft brush.

5 DIY Steps to Fix Poor Cleaning

Step 1: Clean Spray Arms

  1. Remove lower rack
  2. Unscrew or pop off lower spray arm
  3. Remove upper spray arm (varies by model)
  4. Check all spray holes for clogs
  5. Use toothpick to clear each hole
  6. Rinse arms under hot running water
  7. Reinstall and ensure they spin freely

Step 2: Clean Filter Thoroughly

  1. Locate filter at bottom of dishwasher (cylindrical or flat)
  2. Twist and lift to remove
  3. Rinse under hot water
  4. Use soft brush with dish soap to scrub
  5. Check filter housing for debris
  6. Reinstall filter until locked

Step 3: Run Empty Cleaning Cycle

  1. Remove all dishes
  2. Place 2 cups white vinegar in bottom
  3. Run hot water cycle
  4. Sprinkle 1 cup baking soda on bottom
  5. Run short hot cycle
  6. Removes grease, mineral buildup, and odors

Step 4: Check Water Temperature

  1. Run hot water at kitchen sink until hot
  2. Use thermometer to check temp (should be 120¬∞F+)
  3. If below 120¬∞F, increase water heater temp
  4. Always run sink hot water before starting dishwasher

Step 5: Use Proper Detergent and Rinse Aid

  1. Check pod expiration date (old pods lose effectiveness)
  2. Use quality detergent (Cascade, Finish, etc.)
  3. Fill rinse aid dispenser (prevents spots and helps drying)
  4. For hard water, use more detergent or rinse with vinegar
