How to Eliminate Dishwasher Odor

Step 1: Clean Filter Thoroughly

  1. Remove bottom rack
  2. Remove filter (twist counter-clockwise and lift)
  3. Rinse under hot water
  4. Scrub with soft brush and dish soap
  5. Soak in hot water + vinegar for 15 minutes if very dirty
  6. Reinstall filter

Frequency: Weekly to prevent odors

Step 2: Run Vinegar Cleaning Cycle

  1. Remove all dishes
  2. Place 2 cups white vinegar in bowl on top rack
  3. Run hottest, longest cycle
  4. Vinegar breaks down grease, food particles, and soap scum

Cost: $2 (vinegar), Time: 90 minutes

Step 3: Baking Soda Cycle

  1. After vinegar cycle completes
  2. Sprinkle 1 cup baking soda on bottom of dishwasher
  3. Run short hot cycle
  4. Baking soda deodorizes and removes stains

Result: Fresh, odor-free dishwasher

Step 4: Clean Door Gasket and Edges

  1. Wipe door gasket with hot soapy cloth
  2. Clean inside door edges where food hides
  3. Use old toothbrush for crevices
  4. Dry with clean towel

Step 5: Monthly Maintenance

  • Run empty cycle with dishwasher cleaner (Affresh, Finish)
  • Leave door slightly open after cycles to air dry
  • Scrape plates before loading (don't rinse, but remove large food)
  • Run hot water at sink before starting dishwasher

FAQ

Why does my dishwasher smell bad?

Dishwasher smells come from dirty filter (60%), food buildup in door gasket (25%), or bacterial growth in drain hose (10%). Food particles and grease accumulate in filter, creating rotten or fishy smell. Clean filter weekly by removing, scrubbing with dish soap, and rinsing thoroughly. Run monthly vinegar cycle (2 cups vinegar, hot cycle) followed by baking soda cycle (1 cup, short cycle). This DIY deep clean costs $3 and takes 30 minutes active time.
