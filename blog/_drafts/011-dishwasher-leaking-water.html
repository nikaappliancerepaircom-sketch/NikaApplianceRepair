Dishwasher Leaking Water? Find & Fix the Leak

Quick Answer: Dishwasher leaking is caused by worn door gasket (45%), loose drain hose (25%), or faulty pump seal (20%). Door gasket replacement is DIY ($40-$70 part, 30 min). Professional leak repair costs $120-$300 in Toronto. Clean up leaks immediately to prevent floor damage and mold growth.

Common Dishwasher Leak Causes

1. Worn Door Gasket (45%)

Rubber seal around door deteriorates, allowing water to escape during wash cycle.

DIY Fix: Replace door gasket ($40-$70), 30-45 minutes.

2. Loose or Damaged Drain Hose (25%)

Connection loosens or hose cracks, leaking water underneath dishwasher.

DIY Fix: Tighten clamp or replace hose ($15-$30).

3. Faulty Pump Seal (20%)

Pump seal wears out, leaking water from bottom of unit.

Professional Fix: Pump seal/pump replacement ($180-$300).

4. Overfilling or Too Much Detergent

Excessive suds from too much detergent cause overflow.

Fix: Use correct detergent amount, avoid regular dish soap.

5. Unlevel Dishwasher

Tilted dishwasher causes water to pool and leak from door.

DIY Fix: Adjust leveling legs until unit is level.

How to Find Leak Source

  1. Place paper towels around dishwasher (front, sides, back)
  2. Run short wash cycle
  3. Check which towels are wet:
    • Front wet: Door gasket or door latch issue
    • Sides/back wet: Hose connection or pump leak
    • Bottom wet: Pump seal or tub crack
  4. Inspect area where water appears
