Quick Answer: Dishwasher stuck mid-cycle is caused by faulty door latch (35%), heating element failure (25%), or clogged drain preventing cycle advancement (20%). Reset by holding Start/Reset for 3 seconds. If problem persists, door latch replacement costs $100-$180 or heating element costs $150-$250 in Toronto.

Why Dishwasher Won't Complete Cycle

1. Faulty Door Latch (35%)

Door latch sensor fails, dishwasher thinks door is open, pauses cycle.

Test: Open and close door firmly. Check latch for damage.

Professional Fix: Latch replacement ($100-$180).

2. Heating Element Failure (25%)

Dishwasher waits for water to reach temperature but element doesn't heat.

Test: Start cycle, wait 15 min, carefully touch dishes‚Äîshould be warm.

Professional Fix: Element replacement ($150-$250).

3. Drain Not Working (20%)

Water doesn't drain, dishwasher won't advance to next phase.

DIY Fix: Clean filter, check drain hose, run drain cycle.

4. Water Inlet Valve Issue

Valve doesn't fill water, dishwasher waits indefinitely.

Professional Fix: Valve replacement ($120-$200).

5. Control Board Failure

Timer or control board malfunctions, cycle freezes.

Professional Fix: Board replacement ($200-$350).

Quick Fixes to Try

  1. Reset dishwasher: Hold Start/Reset for 3 seconds, or turn off breaker for 5 min
  2. Check door latch: Close door firmly until click
  3. Clean filter: Clogged filter prevents draining and cycle progression
  4. Check water supply: Turn on water supply valve under sink
  5. Run manual drain: Select Cancel/Drain cycle

FAQ

Why won't my dishwasher cycle finish?

Dishwasher won't complete cycle due to faulty door latch (35%), heating element failure (25%), or drainage problem (20%). First, reset by holding Start/Reset 3 seconds. Check that door closes firmly and water drains completely. If dishwasher stops mid-cycle repeatedly, door latch sensor may be failing (Toronto repair: $100-$180). If cycle runs for hours without finishing, heating element may not be working (repair: $150-$250).
