Affresh Cleaner Guide: How to Use for Washers, Dishwashers & More

Affresh appliance cleaners have become a household staple for maintaining washing machines, dishwashers, and garbage disposals. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about using Affresh products effectively, from proper usage techniques to cleaning schedules and troubleshooting common issues.

Your appliances work hard every day, but they can't clean themselves. Over time, washing machines develop odors from trapped residue, dishwashers accumulate mineral deposits and grease, and garbage disposals become breeding grounds for bacteria. Affresh cleaners are specifically formulated to address these problems, keeping your appliances running efficiently and smelling fresh.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about Affresh products, including step-by-step usage instructions for each appliance type, optimal cleaning schedules, and when you might need professional appliance service instead.

What Is Affresh and How Does It Work?

Affresh is a brand of appliance cleaning products owned by Whirlpool Corporation, designed to remove odor-causing residue, limescale, and mineral buildup from household appliances. Unlike regular detergents or household cleaners, Affresh products are formulated to work with the specific materials and mechanisms inside your appliances.

The active ingredients in Affresh cleaners include oxygen-based cleaning agents and surfactants that penetrate and dissolve the biofilm and residue that accumulates in hard-to-reach areas. The tablets are designed to dissolve slowly, allowing the cleaning solution to circulate throughout the entire system during a complete wash cycle.

Pro Tip

While Affresh is a Whirlpool brand, it's safe and effective for use in any brand of washing machine or dishwasher, including Samsung, LG, Maytag, GE, Bosch, and others.

Key Benefits of Using Affresh

  • Eliminates Odors: Removes the musty smell that develops in front-load washers and dishwashers
  • Improves Cleaning Performance: Removes residue that can transfer to clothes or dishes
  • Extends Appliance Life: Prevents mineral buildup that can damage components
  • Easy to Use: No scrubbing required - simply run a cycle
  • Septic Safe: Won't harm septic systems or plumbing

How to Use Affresh Washing Machine Cleaner

Washing machine cleaner is Affresh's most popular product, and for good reason. Modern washing machines, especially high-efficiency front-loaders, are particularly prone to developing odors and residue buildup due to their water-saving designs and airtight door seals.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Top-Load Washers

  1. Ensure the washer is empty: Remove all clothing, towels, and any items from the drum
  2. Place one Affresh tablet: Drop it directly into the bottom of the washer drum, not the detergent dispenser
  3. Select the cleaning cycle: Use the "Clean Washer," "Tub Clean," or "Self Clean" cycle if available. Otherwise, use the hottest and longest regular cycle
  4. Start the cycle: Let the machine run through the complete cycle without interruption
  5. Wipe down the drum: After the cycle completes, wipe the drum with a clean, dry cloth

Step-by-Step Instructions for Front-Load Washers

Front-load washers require slightly different treatment due to their door gasket and different water usage patterns.

  1. Check the door gasket: Pull back the rubber seal and remove any visible debris, hair, or residue
  2. Place one Affresh tablet: Put it directly in the drum, not in any dispenser
  3. Select the cleaning cycle: Use "Clean Washer" or the hottest available cycle
  4. Run the complete cycle: Do not add any clothes or additional products
  5. Clean the gasket: After the cycle, wipe the door gasket thoroughly with a dry cloth
  6. Leave the door open: Allow the drum to air dry to prevent future odor buildup

Important Warning

Never use Affresh tablets with laundry in the machine. The cleaning agents are designed for cleaning the machine itself and can damage fabrics or cause skin irritation if left on clothing.

How to Use Affresh Dishwasher Cleaner

Dishwashers accumulate grease, food particles, and mineral deposits that regular wash cycles don't remove. These buildups can lead to poor cleaning performance, unpleasant odors, and even clogs in the spray arms and drain system.

Dishwasher Cleaning Process

  1. Empty the dishwasher: Remove all dishes, racks are fine to leave in place
  2. Check the filter: Remove and rinse the dishwasher filter under running water
  3. Place the Affresh tablet: Put it in the bottom of the dishwasher, not in the detergent cup
  4. Run a normal cycle: Use the hottest water setting available
  5. Inspect after cleaning: Check spray arms for any remaining debris

Addressing Specific Dishwasher Problems

While Affresh handles general maintenance cleaning, certain issues may require additional attention:

  • Hard Water Deposits: Use Affresh monthly or more frequently in areas with hard water
  • Grease Buildup: Run an additional rinse-only cycle after the cleaning cycle
  • Persistent Odors: Check the garbage disposal connection and air gap for blockages
  • Cloudy Glassware: May indicate the need for water softener adjustment or rinse aid

How to Use Affresh Garbage Disposal Cleaner

Garbage disposals are often overlooked in household cleaning routines, but they can harbor bacteria and develop strong odors. Affresh disposal cleaner uses a foaming action to clean the grinding chamber, splash guard, and drain.

Disposal Cleaning Instructions

  1. Turn on cold water: Run cold water into the disposal
  2. Turn on the disposal: Let it run for a few seconds to clear any debris
  3. Turn off the disposal: Keep the water running
  4. Drop in one Affresh tablet: Place it in the disposal opening
  5. Turn on the disposal: Run for 15 seconds to grind the tablet
  6. Let it foam: The foaming action will clean for 1-2 minutes
  7. Rinse thoroughly: Run cold water and the disposal for 30 seconds

Additional Disposal Maintenance Tips

Between Affresh treatments, you can maintain your disposal with these simple practices:

  • Run cold water before, during, and after using the disposal
  • Avoid putting fibrous foods like celery or corn husks in the disposal
  • Grind citrus peels occasionally for natural freshening
  • Never pour grease or oil into the disposal
  • Clean the splash guard with a brush weekly

Factors That Increase Cleaning Frequency

  • Front-Load Washers: More prone to odor due to sealed design
  • Cold Water Washing: Less effective at dissolving detergent residue
  • Large Families: Higher usage means more buildup
  • Pet Owners: Pet hair and dander contribute to residue

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Affresh Tablet Not Dissolving

If the tablet remains whole or partially dissolved after the cycle:

  • Check that you're using hot water - cold water slows dissolution
  • Ensure the tablet is placed in the drum, not a dispenser
  • Verify the cycle is completing fully without interruption
  • For top-loaders, make sure the water level is sufficient

Odor Persists After Cleaning

If smells continue after using Affresh:

  • Run a second cleaning cycle with another tablet
  • For front-loaders, clean the door gasket manually with diluted bleach
  • Check the drain hose for clogs or kinks
  • Inspect the drain pump filter (front-loaders)
  • Consider professional service if odor persists

When to Call a Professional

Persistent odors despite regular cleaning can indicate a drain problem, mold in hidden areas, or failing seals. These issues require professional diagnosis and repair to prevent water damage and health concerns.

Residue or Film on Clothes After Cleaning

If you notice residue on clothes after running an Affresh cycle:

  • Run an additional rinse cycle before washing clothes
  • Wipe down the drum with a damp cloth
  • Check for undissolved tablet fragments
  • Ensure proper water temperature is being reached

DIY Cleaning Solutions

For those who prefer natural alternatives, these DIY methods can be effective for maintenance cleaning:

Washing Machine - Vinegar and Baking Soda Method

  1. Add 2 cups of white vinegar directly to the drum
  2. Run a hot water cycle
  3. Add 1/2 cup baking soda to the drum
  4. Run another hot water cycle
  5. Wipe down the drum and door seal

Dishwasher - Vinegar Method

  1. Place a cup of white vinegar on the top rack
  2. Run a hot water cycle
  3. Sprinkle baking soda on the bottom
  4. Run a short hot cycle

Pro Tip

DIY methods work well for maintenance but may not be as effective as commercial cleaners for heavy buildup or persistent odors. Consider using Affresh for deep cleaning and DIY methods for between-treatment maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I use Affresh in my washing machine?

Use Affresh washing machine cleaner once per month for optimal results. If you frequently wash heavily soiled loads or use cold water washes, consider using it every 2-3 weeks. Front-load washers may benefit from more frequent cleaning due to their sealed design.

Can I use Affresh with clothes in the washer?

No, never use Affresh with clothes in the washer. The cleaning cycle should be run completely empty to allow the cleaner to work on the drum, seals, and hoses without interference. Running with clothes can cause fabric damage and skin irritation.

Is Affresh safe for septic systems?

Yes, Affresh cleaners are septic-safe when used as directed. The formula is designed to break down properly and won't harm septic system bacteria or your home's plumbing system.

What's the difference between Affresh tablets and powder?

Affresh tablets are designed for deep cleaning cycles and dissolve slowly to clean throughout the entire wash. They work best for monthly maintenance cleaning. Powder cleaners dissolve faster and can be good for quick cleanings but may not penetrate as deeply into seals and hidden areas.

Can I use Affresh in any brand of washing machine?

Yes, Affresh is safe and effective for all brands of washing machines, including Samsung, LG, GE, Maytag, Whirlpool, Bosch, and others. It works in both front-load and top-load models, as well as high-efficiency (HE) machines.

Why does my front-load washer smell even after cleaning?

Persistent odors in front-load washers often indicate mold or mildew in the door gasket or drain system. Try manually cleaning the gasket with diluted bleach, checking the drain pump filter, and leaving the door open between uses. If odor persists, professional service may be needed.

Can I use Affresh dishwasher cleaner in my washing machine?

No, Affresh products are specifically formulated for their intended appliances. Dishwasher cleaner is designed for the different conditions inside a dishwasher. Always use the washing machine cleaner for your washer and dishwasher cleaner for your dishwasher.

Where should I buy Affresh products?

Affresh products are widely available at Canadian Tire, Home Depot, Walmart, Lowe's, and most grocery stores. They're also available online through Amazon.ca and other retailers. Buying in bulk (6-count packages) typically offers the best value.
