Refrigerator Light Not Working? Quick Fix Guide

üìÖ January 20, 2025‚è±Ô∏è 5 min readüìç Toronto
Quick Answer: Refrigerator light not working is caused by burned-out bulb (70%), faulty door switch (20%), or loose socket (10%). Bulb replacement costs $5-$15 and takes 5 minutes (DIY). Door switch replacement costs $80-$150 professionally in Toronto. Always unplug fridge before replacing bulb to avoid electric shock.

How to Fix Refrigerator Light Not Working

Step 1: Replace the Light Bulb (70% of cases)

  1. Unplug refrigerator from wall outlet (SAFETY FIRST)
  2. Locate light bulb (usually top center or sides of fridge)
  3. Remove light cover (twist, pull, or unscrew depending on model)
  4. Unscrew old bulb (turn counter-clockwise)
  5. Check bulb type: A15 appliance bulb (40W max for most models)
  6. Install new appliance-rated bulb (NOT regular household bulb)
  7. Replace cover, plug in fridge, test light

Cost: $5-$15 (appliance bulb)

Time: 5-10 minutes

Important: Use only appliance-rated bulbs‚Äîthey withstand cold temperatures and vibration.

Step 2: Test Door Switch (20% of cases)

  1. Open fridge door fully
  2. Locate door switch (small button on door frame that presses when door closes)
  3. Press switch manually with finger‚Äîlight should turn on
  4. If light doesn't turn on, switch is faulty
  5. Clean around switch (sticky residue can prevent full press)
  6. If cleaning doesn't help, switch needs replacement ($15-$30 part)

Professional repair: $80-$150 (includes labor)

Step 3: Check Light Socket

  1. With fridge unplugged, remove light bulb
  2. Inspect socket for corrosion, burn marks, or loose connections
  3. Clean socket with cotton swab and rubbing alcohol
  4. Check that center contact isn't pushed down (gently pull up if needed)
  5. Install bulb and test

FAQ

What type of bulb does a refrigerator use?

Most refrigerators use A15 appliance bulbs (40W maximum). These special bulbs are designed to withstand cold temperatures and vibration. DO NOT use regular household LED or CFL bulbs‚Äîthey may not work in cold environments or could damage the socket. Check your fridge manual for exact bulb type. Cost: $5-$15 at hardware stores.

Why does my fridge light work sometimes but not always?

Intermittent fridge light indicates a faulty door switch or loose bulb. The door switch may be worn and only makes contact certain ways. Try pressing the switch while door is open‚Äîif light flickers, replace switch. Also check if bulb is screwed in tightly. Vibration can loosen bulbs over time.
