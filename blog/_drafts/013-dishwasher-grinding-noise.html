Causes of Dishwasher Grinding Noise

1. Foreign Object in Pump (45%)

Glass shards, bones, fruit pits, twist ties, labels stuck in pump impeller.

DIY Fix: Turn off power, remove filter, check pump area, remove objects with pliers. $0, 15 min.

2. Worn Pump Bearing (30%)

Pump motor bearing wears out, causing grinding during wash and drain cycles.

Professional Fix: Pump replacement ($180-$320).

3. Damaged Spray Arm (15%)

Spray arm hits racks or has broken bearing, causing grinding as it rotates.

DIY/Pro Fix: Adjust racks away from spray arm, or replace spray arm ($30-$80 DIY, $120-$180 pro).

4. Wash Motor Failure

Wash motor bearing fails or motor struggles, creating grinding sound.

Professional Fix: Motor replacement ($250-$400).

How to Fix Grinding Dishwasher

Step 1: Check for Foreign Objects

  1. Turn off dishwasher power at breaker
  2. Remove bottom rack and spray arm
  3. Remove filter assembly (twist and lift)
  4. Look into pump area with flashlight
  5. Remove any visible objects (glass, plastic, bones, etc.)
  6. Spin pump impeller by hand‚Äîshould turn smoothly
  7. Reinstall filter and spray arm, restore power, test

Success rate: 45% if object found
