5 DIY Fixes for Dishwasher Not Draining

Step 1: Clean the Dishwasher Filter (50% success rate)

  1. Remove bottom dish rack
  2. Locate filter at bottom center of dishwasher (usually cylindrical)
  3. Twist filter counter-clockwise and lift out
  4. Rinse filter under hot water, use soft brush to remove debris
  5. Check for food particles, broken glass, or labels
  6. Reinstall filter by placing and twisting clockwise until locked
  7. Run short cycle to test

Cost: $0 (DIY)

Time: 10-15 minutes

Prevents: 50% of drain issues if done monthly

Step 2: Check Drain Hose for Clogs

  1. Turn off water supply and power to dishwasher
  2. Pull dishwasher out from under counter
  3. Locate drain hose (corrugated hose running to sink or garbage disposal)
  4. Disconnect hose from dishwasher and disposal/sink drain
  5. Run water through hose‚Äîshould flow freely
  6. If clogged, use plumber's snake or straightened coat hanger
  7. Reconnect hose ensuring no kinks or high loops

Cost: $0 (DIY)

Time: 30-45 minutes

Step 3: Check Garbage Disposal Connection

If dishwasher drains into garbage disposal:

  1. Turn off power to disposal
  2. Check if disposal is clogged‚Äîrun disposal to clear
  3. Look for knockout plug (new disposals have plug in dishwasher inlet)
  4. If plug wasn't removed during installation, dishwasher can't drain
  5. Remove plug from inside disposal inlet with hammer and screwdriver

Cost: $0

Note: Knockout plug is #1 cause in new disposal installations

Step 4: Clean Drain Pump and Check for Obstructions

  1. Remove standing water with cup or wet-dry vacuum
  2. Remove filter and filter housing
  3. Look into drain pump area with flashlight
  4. Check for broken glass, twist ties, labels, bones
  5. Remove any visible obstructions with pliers
  6. Spin pump impeller by hand‚Äîshould turn freely

Warning: Turn off power before reaching into pump area

Step 5: Run Drain Cycle Reset

  1. Cancel current cycle
  2. Open door and press Start/Reset button for 3 seconds
  3. Close door and select "Drain" or "Cancel/Drain" cycle
  4. Let cycle complete (3-5 minutes)
  5. If water drains, issue may have been temporary clog

FAQ

Why is my dishwasher not draining?

Dishwasher not draining is caused by clogged filter (50% of cases), blocked drain hose (25%), faulty drain pump (15%), or air gap/disposal clog (10%). Start by cleaning the filter at bottom of dishwasher‚Äîthis fixes most issues. If filter is clean, check drain hose for kinks or clogs. Standing water also attracts bacteria and causes odors within 24 hours, so fix promptly. Professional repair in Toronto costs $120-$300 depending on cause.

Can I run dishwasher if it's not draining?

No, do not run dishwasher if previous water hasn't drained. Running another cycle will add more water, causing overflow and potential floor damage. Standing water also breeds bacteria and mold. Fix the drainage issue first, then run empty cycle with vinegar to sanitize.
