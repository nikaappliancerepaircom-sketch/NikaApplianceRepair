Why Won't My Washing Machine Drain?

1. Clogged Drain Pump Filter (45%)

Lint, coins, bobby pins, and small items clog pump filter, preventing drainage.

DIY Fix: Clean pump filter (front access panel), remove debris. $0, 20 min.

2. Kinked or Clogged Drain Hose (25%)

Drain hose bent, kinked, or clogged with lint buildup blocks water flow.

DIY Fix: Straighten hose, remove clogs with plumber's snake. $0-$15.

3. Faulty Drain Pump (20%)

Pump motor fails or impeller breaks, preventing water from being pumped out.

Professional Fix: Drain pump replacement ($180-$320).

4. Lid Switch Failure (Top Loaders)

Faulty lid switch prevents drain/spin cycle from running (safety feature).

Professional Fix: Lid switch replacement ($120-$200).

5. Clogged Standpipe or Drain

House drain pipe clogged, causing water to back up into washer.

DIY/Pro Fix: Snake standpipe, or call plumber if main drain clogged.

6 DIY Steps to Fix Washer Not Draining

Step 1: Clean Drain Pump Filter

For Front Loaders:

  1. Locate small access panel at bottom front (usually bottom right)
  2. Place towels on floor (water will spill)
  3. Open panel, pull out small drain hose (if present)
  4. Place bucket under hose, remove cap, let water drain
  5. Twist pump filter counter-clockwise and pull out
  6. Remove lint, coins, bobby pins, small items
  7. Clean filter under running water
  8. Reinstall filter (twist clockwise until tight), replace panel

Success rate: 45% if filter was clogged

Step 2: Check Drain Hose

  1. Pull washer away from wall
  2. Locate drain hose (usually 1.5" corrugated hose)
  3. Check for kinks or sharp bends
  4. Disconnect hose from standpipe/drain
  5. Run water through hose‚Äîshould flow freely
  6. If clogged, use plumber's snake or high-pressure water
  7. Reconnect, ensure no kinks

Step 3: Test Drain Pump

  1. Start drain/spin cycle
  2. Listen for drain pump running (humming sound from bottom)
  3. If pump runs but no drainage: clog in pump or hose
  4. If pump is silent: pump motor failed or electrical issue
  5. If pump hums but doesn't pump: impeller jammed or broken

Step 4: Check Standpipe

  1. Remove drain hose from standpipe
  2. Pour bucket of water down standpipe
  3. Should drain quickly (within 5 seconds)
  4. If drains slowly or backs up, standpipe is clogged
  5. Use plumber's snake to clear standpipe

Step 5: Manually Drain Washer

If washer won't drain and needs immediate emptying:

  1. Front loaders: Use drain hose from pump filter access panel
  2. Top loaders: Use wet-dry vacuum or siphon with hose
  3. Bail water with cups into buckets
  4. Remove clothes after draining (will be soaking wet)
  5. Wring out in bathtub or run through spin cycle at laundromat
