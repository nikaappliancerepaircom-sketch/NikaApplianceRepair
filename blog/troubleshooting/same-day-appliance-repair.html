Common Same-Day Emergency Repairs

Understanding what commonly breaks and what the repair process looks like helps you know what to expect. These are the appliance emergencies we handle most frequently with same-day response.

Refrigerator Leaking or Not Cooling

A refrigerator that stops cooling is always an emergency. Diagnoses range from a failed compressor (requiring replacement, $400-$600) to a simple clogged drain line (repair, $150-$250). A leaking fridge usually indicates a frozen or blocked drain tube, cracked evaporator pan, or malfunctioning water inlet valve. Same-day response prevents food spoilage and health risks. We arrive with replacement parts for these common failures and typically complete repairs the same day.

Washing Machine Overflowing

Washing machine emergencies cause immediate water damage. Common causes include a faulty water inlet valve, broken pump, or malfunctioning control board. Fill valve replacement typically costs $200-$300 and can be completed in one visit. Pump failures require $300-$500 in repairs. The key to protecting your home is stopping water flow immediately and getting a technician to properly diagnose and repair the cause. We prioritize washer emergencies and typically respond within 2-3 hours.

Oven Not Heating

An electric oven failing to heat typically indicates a broken heating element ($150-$250) or failed thermal fuse ($100-$150). Gas ovens might have an igniter problem ($200-$350) or a faulty safety valve. Most oven heating failures can be repaired same-day, though we sometimes need to order parts for specialty models. Same-day diagnosis at minimum gets you a clear understanding of the problem and timeline to repair.

Dryer Not Heating or Running

Dryers that don't heat usually need a new thermal fuse ($100-$200), heating element ($200-$400), or thermostat ($150-$300). Dryers that won't start at all might have a broken door latch ($150-$250) or failed start switch ($100-$200). These are straightforward repairs that our technicians complete in one visit in most cases. Same-day dryer repair prevents laundry buildup and routine disruption for families.

Before Service Arrives: What You Should Do

After calling for emergency repair and receiving an arrival window, there are important actions to take while waiting for our technician. These steps protect your home, prevent additional damage, and help our technician work efficiently.

Immediate Safety Precautions

For electrical appliances: Unplug the malfunctioning appliance immediately. A refrigerator leaking water while plugged in creates shock hazards. An oven or dryer showing sparks or burning smells should be unplugged and the circuit breaker switched off. Safety comes first‚Äînever attempt to operate an appliance behaving dangerously.

For appliances with water: If water is actively flowing, shut off the water supply to that appliance if you can locate the valve. For washing machines or dishwashers, this is usually a shutoff valve behind or beside the appliance. Turning off the supply stops water from continuing to leak during your wait for service. Don't attempt repairs yourself if you're unsure‚Äîeven trying to stop water flow can sometimes cause more damage.

For gas appliances: If you smell gas or suspect a gas leak, evacuate immediately and call emergency services (911). Don't attempt any repairs or diagnosis involving gas appliances. Our technicians handle gas repair calls with appropriate safety protocols.

Information to Have Ready

When our technician arrives, they'll work faster and more effectively if you have this information available:

  • Appliance brand, model number, and serial number (usually found on a sticker inside or on the back)
  • When you first noticed the problem and what was happening at that moment
  • Any recent repairs or service calls for this appliance
  • Whether the problem started suddenly or gradually developed
  • Any unusual noises, smells, or visible damage

Temporary Troubleshooting (Without Taking Risks)

While waiting for our technician, some safe preliminary checks might help: If a refrigerator won't cool, check that the thermostat isn't set to the warmest setting and that nothing is blocking the air vents inside. If a washer won't drain, check that the drain hose isn't kinked or clogged with lint. If an oven won't heat, verify the circuit breaker hasn't tripped. These simple checks might reveal the problem is easily fixable. But if you're uncertain or if these checks don't help, stop troubleshooting and wait for professional diagnosis.
