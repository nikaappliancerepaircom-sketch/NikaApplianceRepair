Water Quality Impact: Hard Water and Appliance Wear in Peterborough

Understanding Peterborough's Hard Water

Peterborough's water supply contains minerals—primarily calcium and magnesium—that create what experts call "hard water." With hardness levels between 200-300 parts per million (ppm), Peterborough has water quality similar to Toronto and other major Ontario cities. While this water is safe to drink and use, it significantly impacts your home appliances. Hard water is one of the leading causes of appliance failure in the region, accelerating wear on critical components in washing machines, dishwashers, refrigerators, and water heaters.

How Hard Water Damages Peterborough Appliances

Mineral deposits build up on heating elements, pipes, and internal components of your appliances. In washing machines, hard water causes mineral buildup that reduces water flow and heating efficiency, leading to poor cleaning performance and higher energy bills. Dishwashers experience spotting and film buildup on dishes, and their spray arms can become clogged with mineral deposits. Refrigerators with water dispensers and ice makers accumulate mineral scale in the water line, eventually causing leaks or complete blockages. Water heaters are particularly vulnerable, with scale buildup reducing heating efficiency and lifespan.

Seasonal Challenges in Peterborough

Winter Stress (November-March)

During Peterborough's cold winters, appliances work harder. Heating systems run continuously, washing machine water supply lines can freeze, and condensation from temperature extremes can cause electrical issues. We see a spike in water heater repairs and frozen water line emergencies during winter months.

Summer Strain (June-August)

Summer brings different challenges. Refrigerators and air conditioning units work at maximum capacity, and outdoor compressor units experience stress from heat and humidity. Hard water evaporation becomes more pronounced during summer, leaving even heavier mineral deposits on components.

Spring Thaw (April-May)

As winter ends, pipes that have been frozen can develop issues. Water pressure fluctuations during spring thaw can affect appliance water inlets and cause leaks that we frequently encounter in Peterborough homes.

Common Peterborough Appliance Issues We Fix Daily

In our years serving Peterborough residents, we've encountered virtually every appliance problem. Here are the most common issues we see and how they relate to Peterborough's specific conditions:

Washing Machine Problems (Hard Water Focus)

Washing machine repairs are our most frequent service calls in Peterborough. Hard water buildup is the primary culprit. Symptoms include water not draining properly, clothes still smelling after washing, reduced water flow, and the machine making grinding noises. Hard water minerals accumulate in the drum seal, water inlet valve, and drainage pump. We often find that drain hoses are clogged with mineral deposits, and the inlet water screens are completely blocked. We recommend cleaning or replacing these components annually in areas with Peterborough's water hardness level.

Dishwasher Issues

Dishwashers in Peterborough frequently suffer from hard water-related problems. You might notice dishes coming out still dirty, with spots and film, or the dishwasher not filling with enough water. The circulation spray arm jets become blocked with mineral deposits, reducing water pressure. The heating element also accumulates scale, reducing its efficiency and causing the dishwasher to struggle to reach proper washing temperatures. Regular descaling and filter maintenance can extend your dishwasher's lifespan significantly.

Refrigerator Concerns

Refrigerator problems in Peterborough often relate to the water and ice systems. If your ice maker is producing less ice or no ice at all, or if your water dispenser is weak or stopped working, hard water mineral deposits in the water supply line are likely the cause. These deposits accumulate gradually, restricting water flow until the system eventually fails. We frequently replace clogged water inlet valves and water lines in Peterborough refrigerators. We also see issues with compressor failure due to hard water affecting cooling efficiency, and frost accumulation problems during winter months when temperature fluctuations are dramatic.

Seasonal Heating System Needs

While water heaters are essential year-round, Peterborough's cold winters mean they work much harder from November through March. We see increased water heater repair requests during winter, primarily due to mineral scale buildup reducing efficiency and lifespan. A water heater that works efficiently in summer may struggle to keep up with winter demand. Regular maintenance and descaling can keep your water heater running reliably through Peterborough's challenging winters.
