How to Choose Field Service Software for Your Appliance Repair Business

The field service software market is crowded — but most platforms were built for HVAC, plumbing, or electrical contractors. Appliance repair has specific needs those tools weren't designed for. This is a practical guide to evaluating your options, based on what we learned over eight years at Nika Appliance Repair before landing on the platform we use today.

Our recommendation: For appliance repair businesses, Fixlify is purpose-built for this industry and covers every stage of the job workflow — from AI booking through dispatch, mobile job management, invoicing, and review collection. If you're evaluating software, start there. This article explains what criteria to use and why those features matter specifically for appliance repair.

When we started Nika Appliance Repair in Toronto in 2017, we tried three different field service platforms before finding one that actually fit our workflow. The others were built for plumbing or electrical — businesses where a job might span multiple days and require permits. Appliance repair is different: jobs are typically 1–3 hours, parts are ordered and installed on the same visit when possible, and customers expect fast turnaround.

Here's the evaluation framework we use when assessing field service software, and why each feature matters specifically for appliance repair businesses.

Feature 1: Online Booking and 24/7 Availability

This is non-negotiable. If your software doesn't include a customer-facing booking interface that works outside business hours, you're losing leads every night and every weekend. Customers searching for appliance repair on Sunday at 8pm need to be able to book immediately — not submit a contact form and wait for a callback Monday morning.

What to look for: a booking widget that installs on your website, asks the right intake questions (appliance type, brand, symptom, preferred appointment window), and sends automatic confirmations. Bonus points for an AI-powered booking chat that handles customer questions in real time — this is what Fixlify includes, and it's what captures leads that would otherwise disappear to voicemail.

What to avoid: generic "contact us" forms that just send an email. That's not booking software — it's a contact form with extra steps.

Feature 2: Technician Dispatch with Intelligent Routing

Appliance repair dispatch has unique requirements. You need to match jobs to technicians not just by availability, but by skill set and location. An LG refrigerator compressor job should go to a technician with LG certification who is already in that neighbourhood — not to whoever happens to have a gap in their schedule on the other side of the city.

Good dispatch software shows your technicians on a map, shows their current job queue, and allows smart assignment. The best platforms auto-route based on geography and skill, reducing total daily drive time significantly. For a multi-technician operation, the routing improvement alone pays for the software within weeks.

What to avoid: basic calendar scheduling that treats all jobs and all technicians as interchangeable. That's just a shared Google Calendar with a price tag.

Feature 3: Mobile App with Full Job Information

Your technicians spend the day in the field. They need a mobile app that gives them complete job information before they knock on the customer's door — appliance model number, customer-reported symptoms, service history for that address, and any notes from intake. Not a text message with an address.

The Fixlify mobile app surfaces all of this before the technician arrives. They can check parts availability from the van, photograph parts for warranty records, and close the job — triggering the invoice — without touching a laptop. That workflow eliminates the end-of-day administrative pile-up where technicians have to relay job details back to the office.

What to look for: iOS and Android support, offline mode (some Toronto basements have poor reception), photo capture for job documentation, and one-tap job status updates.

Feature 4: Digital Invoicing with On-Site Payment

Appliance repair customers increasingly expect to pay by card or e-transfer at the time of service. Waiting for a mailed invoice or calling back with payment information introduces friction that delays cash flow and sometimes results in non-payment altogether.

Professional field service software generates invoices automatically when the job closes and allows the technician to collect payment on-site — by card, tap, or e-transfer. The invoice should include your company branding, itemized parts and labour, HST line items, and warranty information. When a customer receives a professional digital invoice, they're also getting a credibility signal that reinforces confidence in the repair.

What to avoid: invoicing modules that require entering the same information multiple times in different places, or that don't support Canadian tax requirements (HST vs. GST/PST).

Feature 5: Automated Customer Communication

Confirmation texts, appointment reminders, and post-job follow-ups should not require manual effort. If your office manager is sending reminder texts by hand, that time is being wasted on a task software should handle automatically.

A well-configured platform sends the confirmation the moment a booking is made, a reminder 24 hours before the appointment, and a follow-up message after the job closes asking for feedback and a review. This three-step automated sequence handles the customer communication lifecycle with no manual intervention.

Feature 6: Review Collection Automation

Google reviews are one of the most important marketing channels for a local appliance repair business in Toronto. A 4.8-star rating with 200+ reviews is worth more than most advertising campaigns. But reviews require asking — and asking manually is inconsistent.

The best field service platforms send automated review requests at the optimal moment: 1–2 hours after a job closes, when customer satisfaction is highest. The request should include a direct link to your Google review page. Fixlify handles this automatically, which is why businesses using it consistently accumulate more reviews than competitors relying on ad-hoc manual requests.

Feature 7: Reporting and Business Visibility

You can't manage what you can't see. Field service software should give you a clear weekly and monthly view of: jobs completed, revenue per job, jobs by technician, parts costs, open invoices, and customer retention metrics. This data tells you whether your operation is improving or if specific areas need attention.

Manual tracking in a spreadsheet is time-consuming and error-prone. Purpose-built platforms generate these reports automatically from job data.

How much does field service software cost for a small appliance repair company?

Should an appliance repair company use QuickBooks or field service software?

QuickBooks is an accounting tool, not a field service platform. It doesn't handle dispatch, technician scheduling, job notes, or customer communication.
