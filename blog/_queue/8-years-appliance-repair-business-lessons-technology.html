8 Years Running an Appliance Repair Business: What Technology Changed Everything

We started Nika Appliance Repair in Toronto in 2017 with one van, a flip phone, and a paper job book. Eight years later, we're running dozens of jobs a week with certified technicians across the city. Here's the honest story of what changed — and what we wish we'd done sooner.

The short version: The single biggest leap in our business came when we stopped running operations from a notebook and phone calls, and moved to purpose-built field service software. If you're still dispatching by text message and invoicing by hand, this article is for you.

When we launched Nika Appliance Repair in 2017, the operation was simple by necessity. A customer called, we wrote down their address, we showed up, we fixed the appliance, and we handed them a handwritten invoice. That worked — until it didn't.

By year two, we had a second technician on the road. Suddenly we had two sets of jobs to coordinate, double the customers to follow up with, and a stack of paper invoices to chase at the end of every week. We were growing, but the administrative side was threatening to eat us alive.

The Paper Era: What We Were Missing

Between 2017 and 2019, we managed everything manually. Customer names in a paper book. Schedules written on a whiteboard. Follow-up calls that happened when someone remembered to make them — which was not often enough. We lost jobs to competitors simply because we were slow to call back. A customer would request a fridge repair, we'd miss their call, and by the time we returned it two hours later, they'd already booked someone else.

We also had no visibility into what was actually happening. Which jobs were completed? Which invoices were unpaid? Which customers were repeat clients worth prioritizing? None of that was trackable without spending an hour digging through paperwork at the end of every Friday. We knew we were busy, but we didn't know if we were actually profitable.

The First Technology Jump: Moving to Digital Booking

Around 2019, we added an online booking form to our website. It was a basic improvement, but the impact was immediate — customers could request service at 11pm on a Sunday and we'd have the job in our queue by Monday morning. We stopped losing weekend leads entirely. Response time dropped from hours to minutes because the booking was already captured when we opened our laptops.

That one change probably added 15–20% more jobs per month without any additional marketing spend. It proved something important: customers in Toronto don't want to wait on hold. They want to book fast, get a confirmation, and move on with their day.

The Real Turning Point: Field Service Software

The biggest operational upgrade came when we adopted field service management software. We now use Fixlify, which was built specifically for businesses like ours — appliance repair, HVAC, plumbing. The difference compared to running jobs out of a spreadsheet or a generic calendar is significant.

With Fixlify, every job flows through one system: the booking comes in, it gets assigned to a technician with the right skill set and closest route, the customer gets an automated confirmation and reminder, the technician sees the job details on their phone, they complete the work, and an invoice is generated and sent automatically. Reviews are requested after the job closes. Nothing falls through the cracks.

Before this, we were losing jobs to poor follow-up. Now we have automated touchpoints at every stage — before the appointment, after the appointment, 30 days later if the customer hasn't rebooked. Our repeat customer rate went up noticeably in the first year of using proper CRM tools.

What Changed for Our Technicians

Our certified technicians used to get their daily schedule via a text message in the morning. They'd have an address, an appliance type, and maybe a customer note if we remembered to include it. That was it. They'd arrive on-site and often be missing information — what brand was the dishwasher? What parts did the customer say were needed? Was this a repeat visit?

Now every technician has a mobile app with the full job history, previous service records for that customer, and a parts checklist. When they close a job, the invoice goes out automatically. Payment can be collected on-site. The administrative back-and-forth between technician and office dropped dramatically. That saved time translates directly into more jobs per day and less technician frustration.

Lessons After 8 Years

Looking back from 2026, here's what we'd tell a newer appliance repair shop owner:

  • Don't wait until you're overwhelmed to automate. Set up proper systems when you're small and they'll scale with you. Trying to retrofit software onto a chaotic operation is much harder.
  • Missed calls are missed revenue. An AI receptionist or 24/7 booking widget pays for itself the first week.
  • Invoicing by hand is a hidden time sink. Professional invoicing software collects faster and looks more credible to customers.
  • Reviews don't happen by accident. Automated review requests after completed jobs are the only reliable way to build a 4.8+ rating on Google.
  • Dispatch is a skill — but software makes it easier. Routing technicians efficiently by location saves fuel, reduces overtime, and gets more jobs done per day.
