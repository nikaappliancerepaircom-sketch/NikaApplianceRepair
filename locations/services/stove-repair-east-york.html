Common Stove & Range Repair Problems We Fix in East York

Older narrow kitchen layouts are common in East York bungalows. Some homes have 60-amp panels and basement laundry with limited ventilation.

Burner Not Igniting

Gas burner clicking but not lighting, or electric coil not heating. Clogged igniter ports, failed igniter switch, or burnt-out burner element are common causes. We clean ports and replace igniters.

Burner Heating Unevenly

Hot spots or cool zones on the cooking surface. Partially clogged gas ports or a failing electric element cause uneven heating. We clean burner systems and replace elements as needed.

Stove Control Knob Broken

Knob loose, cracked, or not controlling the burner properly. We source manufacturer-matched replacement knobs and repair the underlying valve or switch if needed.

Gas Smell from Stove

Smell of gas near the range at any time. This requires immediate attention. We check all gas connections, valve seals, and burner fittings for leaks. Do not use the stove until this is resolved.

Electric Coil Burner Broken

Coil element not glowing, sparking, or broken in two. Element or drip bowl replacements are quick repairs. We stock common coil sizes for same-day replacement.

Stove Control Board Fault

Display errors, buttons not responding, or digital controls behaving erratically. Control board failures are often repairable without full replacement. We diagnose and advise the most cost-effective fix.

What are the most common stove problems in East York homes?

East York's post-war bungalow stock means older GE and Kenmore gas ranges are the dominant appliance here — many of these homes have had the same gas range for 15 or more years. The most frequent service call we get from East York is bake igniter failure: the oven stops heating entirely, but the surface burners still work fine. The bake igniter glows orange when you turn the oven on but is too weak to open the gas safety valve — it needs current above a threshold to trigger the valve, and as igniters age they fall below that threshold. Igniter electrode wear on the surface burners is the second most common call: the burner clicks repeatedly but will not light, or requires multiple attempts. Thermostat drift is third — the oven runs noticeably hotter or cooler than the set temperature, which East York residents often notice when longtime recipes start coming out differently. Kenmore gas models from the late 2000s and early 2010s are particularly prone to all three of these issues.
