Common Refrigerator Repair Problems We Fix in Little Italy

Apartment and student housing often has older appliances requiring part sourcing. Some buildings have landlord-supplied appliances in need of repair.

Fridge Not Cooling

Food spoiling despite the compressor running. Dirty condenser coils, a failed evaporator fan, broken thermostat, or refrigerant leak are common causes. We carry diagnostic tools and parts for same-day resolution.

Freezer Not Freezing

Ice cream going soft or frozen food thawing. Usually a defrost system failure including the defrost heater, thermostat, or timer. We test the full defrost circuit and replace the faulty component on-site.

Ice Build-Up on Coils

Frost accumulation blocking airflow and stopping cooling. A classic defrost failure. We defrost the unit and repair the underlying component causing the recurring ice build-up.

Water Leaking Inside or Under

Water in the crisper drawer or on your floor. Causes include a blocked defrost drain tube, a cracked water line to the ice maker, or a damaged door seal. We clear blockages and replace seals or water lines.

Ice Maker Not Working

No ice production. Frozen fill tubes, broken ice maker assemblies, or faulty water inlet valves are typical causes. We restore ice production with OEM-compatible replacement parts.

Fridge Making Noise

Buzzing, clicking, or grinding from the unit. Failing condenser fan motors, noisy compressors, or rattling evaporator fans are common. We identify the source and repair it cleanly.

What are the most common refrigerator problems in Little Italy homes?

The number one refrigerator problem in Little Italy is dirty condenser coils. The older GE, Whirlpool, and Hotpoint models in the College and Bathurst Street homes accumulate years of pet hair and household dust on the coil fins at the bottom or rear of the unit. This buildup blocks airflow and causes the compressor to overheat, leading to a fridge that runs constantly, feels warm inside, and eventually stops cooling altogether. A thorough condenser coil cleaning ($100-150) resolves this fully in the vast majority of cases and is one of the most satisfying repairs we do. Door seal deterioration is second: the rubber gaskets on older units crack and lose their seal, allowing warm air to enter continuously. Third is thermostat failure, which presents as inconsistent temperatures throughout the fridge. We also see compressor start relay (PTC relay) failures on older GE units, causing a clicking sound every few minutes.
