Common Dryer Repair Problems We Fix in Dufferin Grove

Mixed housing stock means varied appliance ages. Some older rental units have appliances approaching end-of-life. Condo units require building access coordination.

Dryer Not Heating

Clothes coming out damp after a full cycle. Blown thermal fuse, failed heating element, or a bad cycling thermostat are most common. We carry heating elements for all major brands.

Dryer Not Turning

Drum stationary when the cycle starts. A broken drive belt is the most frequent cause, followed by a failed motor or idler pulley. Belt replacements take 30 to 60 minutes.

Dryer Takes Too Long

Multiple cycles needed to dry a single load. Clogged exhaust duct or a failing heating element are almost always responsible. We clean ducts and replace parts in one visit.

Dryer Making Loud Noise

Squealing, thumping, or grinding during operation. Worn drum glides, damaged drum seal, or failing drum bearings are typical. We replace worn components before they cause further damage.

Dryer Not Starting

No response when the start button is pressed. Door switch, start switch, motor, or thermal fuse failures cause this. We test the full electrical circuit and replace the faulty component.

Dryer Overheating

Drum excessively hot or the unit shutting off mid-cycle. A blocked exhaust duct or failed high-limit thermostat causes overheating. This is a fire risk and should be addressed promptly.

What are the most common dryer problems in Dufferin Grove homes?

Heating element failure is the leading dryer complaint we receive from Dufferin Grove, especially in apartment buildings where machines see heavy use from renters. Electric dryers running 8-12 cycles per week put the nichrome heating element under sustained stress; elements typically last 6-9 years under high use but can fail earlier if the vent is partially restricted. Heating element replacement costs $150-$250 and is usually completed same-day. The second most common problem is control board errors on LG and Samsung dryers — these appear as error codes (d80, d90, d95 on LG; HE, HC on Samsung) and are often triggered by overheating from a lint-blocked vent rather than a true board failure. Clearing the vent first sometimes resolves the code. The third most common call is thermal fuse failure from lint buildup in the short apartment vent run.
