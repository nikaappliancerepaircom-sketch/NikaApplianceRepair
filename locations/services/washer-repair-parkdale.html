Common Washer Problems We Fix in Parkdale

From older top-load machines to modern front-loaders, Parkdale's washing machine issues are as varied as its housing stock. Our technicians solve them all.

Washer Not Draining

Standing water left after the wash cycle is usually a clogged pump filter, blocked drain hose, or failed drain pump. In Parkdale's older top-loaders, pump filters are often neglected for years. We clear blockages and replace failed pumps for complete drainage restoration.

Excessive Vibration and Banging

Washing machines that shake violently or bang against walls are a problem in Parkdale's multi-unit buildings. Causes include worn drum bearings, broken suspension rods, failing shock absorbers, or unbalanced loads. We balance the machine and replace worn support components.

Front-Loader Door Boot Leaks

Front-load washers leak from the door boot seal when it cracks or tears. This is especially common in older front-loaders installed in Parkdale renovations. We replace door boots with OEM parts and inspect the door latch and hinge at the same visit.

Washer Won't Start or Fill

A washer that won't start or fails to fill with water indicates control board issues, failed water inlet valves, or a triggered lid switch safety mechanism. We diagnose the electrical and mechanical cause and restore full operation in one service call.

Mouldy Drum Odour (Front-Loaders)

Front-load washers in Parkdale rentals often develop mould odours from closed door use and low-temperature washing. We deep-clean the drum and gasket, treat the mould professionally, and advise on prevention routines to keep the unit fresh going forward.

Spin Cycle Not Working

Clothes coming out soaking wet means the spin cycle isn't completing. This often points to a failed lid switch on top-loaders, worn carbon brushes on the motor, or a broken drive belt. We carry these parts for same-day resolution.
