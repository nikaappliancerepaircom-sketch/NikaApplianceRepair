Common Dryer Problems We Fix in Richmond Hill

From vent blockages to failed heating elements, our Richmond Hill technicians fix most dryer faults in a single visit.

Dryer Not Heating

Drum spins but clothes remain damp after a full cycle. A blown heating element or thermal fuse (electric dryers), or a faulty igniter or gas valve solenoid (gas dryers) are typical causes. We test and replace the failed component on-site within the same visit.

Blocked Exhaust Vent

Richmond Hill's detached homes have longer vent paths than condos, making lint buildup more frequent and more dangerous. A blocked vent causes slow drying and is the leading residential dryer fire cause. We clear blockages and test airflow velocity on every call.

Dryer Won't Start

No response when you press start. A faulty door switch, blown thermal cutoff fuse, failed start relay, or bad control board are typical culprits. We test all relevant electrical components and replace only what's failed.

Drum Not Turning

The motor runs, the dryer heats, but the drum won't rotate. A broken drum belt is the most common cause — usually from heavy, frequent loads common in large Richmond Hill households. We also check idler pulleys and drum glides.

Dryer Overheats or Shuts Off

Dryer runs briefly then cuts out, or clothes come out unusually hot. The high-limit thermostat tripped from restricted exhaust airflow. We clear the duct, replace thermostats, and ensure the dryer restarts safely before leaving.

Loud Noise During Operation

Squealing, thumping, or rumbling noises during the cycle. Worn drum glides, failed drum bearing, or a broken idler pulley are typical causes. We disassemble and replace worn drum support components to restore quiet operation.
