Common Oven Problems We Fix in Richmond Hill

From a gas burner that won't light to an oven that won't hold temperature, our Richmond Hill technicians fix most faults in a single visit.

Oven Not Reaching Temperature

The oven preheats but never reaches the set temperature, causing undercooked food. In gas ovens — common for Persian baking in Richmond Hill — a failing igniter that takes too long to heat loses enough gas before lighting, reducing burner output. We replace weak igniters and test temperature accuracy on-site.

Gas Burner Won't Ignite

The click of the igniter sounds but no flame appears, or the burner ignites but won't stay lit. With the frequency of stovetop cooking in many Richmond Hill households, igniters and burner caps accumulate food residue that blocks gas flow and electricity to the spark. We clean, test, and replace igniter components same-day.

Uneven Baking

Food comes out burnt on one side and undercooked on the other. A partially failed bake element, a faulty temperature sensor, or a convection fan that has stopped circulating hot air evenly are typical causes. We test and replace the faulty component to restore even heat distribution.

Oven Won't Turn On

No response when you turn the oven on. A blown thermal fuse, faulty ignition switch, failed control board, or tripped circuit breaker are typical causes. We test all electrical pathways and identify the root fault rather than replacing parts unnecessarily.

Oven Door Won't Seal

Visible heat escaping from the oven door, or the door droops and doesn't sit flush. A worn door gasket or broken door hinge springs are the most common causes. We replace door seals and hinges on-site to restore efficient, controlled baking temperatures.

Self-Clean Lock-Up

Oven locked after a self-clean cycle and won't unlock. A failed door lock motor or a control board that didn't register cycle completion is typically at fault. We reset and replace the lock mechanism to restore full oven access safely.
