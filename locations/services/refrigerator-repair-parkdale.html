Common Refrigerator Problems We Fix in Parkdale

Parkdale's older housing stock and diverse appliance ages mean our technicians see a wide range of refrigerator faults — from vintage compressor failures to modern smart fridge electronics.

Older Fridge Not Cooling Properly

Pre-2010 refrigerators in Parkdale apartments frequently develop thermostat drift, failing start relays, or sealed-system refrigerant issues. We diagnose and repair older units efficiently, sourcing legacy parts to keep functioning appliances running without forcing unnecessary replacements.

Door Seal Deterioration

Parkdale's older fridges suffer from dried, cracked, or compressed door gaskets that allow warm air infiltration. This forces the compressor to run constantly, spiking electricity bills and wearing out the unit faster. We replace door seals same-day for most common refrigerator models.

Compressor Running Constantly

A refrigerator that never stops running is wasting electricity and will fail soon. Common causes are dirty condenser coils, a failed thermostat, or a leaking door gasket. We inspect the full system, clean coils, and replace faulty components to restore efficient cycling.

Ice Build-Up and Frost Problems

Manual-defrost freezers common in older Parkdale apartments develop heavy frost build-up. Automatic-defrost fridges with defrost system failures also accumulate ice on the evaporator coil. We fix defrost heaters, defrost thermostats, and timers to stop ice build-up permanently.

Water Pooling in Crisper Drawer

Water collecting in the bottom of the fridge compartment usually points to a blocked defrost drain. The drain tube freezes or clogs with food debris, causing meltwater to overflow into the crisper. We clear and treat the drain, then test to confirm proper drainage.

Loud Humming or Buzzing Noises

Noisy refrigerators disturb Parkdale's often close-quarters living arrangements. Failing condenser or evaporator fan motors, vibrating compressors, or loose components are common noise sources. We identify the cause and repair or replace the noisy part for quiet, reliable operation.
