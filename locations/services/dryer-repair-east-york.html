Common Dryer Repair Problems We Fix in East York

Older narrow kitchen layouts are common in East York bungalows. Some homes have 60-amp panels and basement laundry with limited ventilation.

Dryer Not Heating

Clothes coming out damp after a full cycle. Blown thermal fuse, failed heating element, or a bad cycling thermostat are most common. We carry heating elements for all major brands.

Dryer Not Turning

Drum stationary when the cycle starts. A broken drive belt is the most frequent cause, followed by a failed motor or idler pulley. Belt replacements take 30 to 60 minutes.

Dryer Takes Too Long

Multiple cycles needed to dry a single load. Clogged exhaust duct or a failing heating element are almost always responsible. We clean ducts and replace parts in one visit.

Dryer Making Loud Noise

Squealing, thumping, or grinding during operation. Worn drum glides, damaged drum seal, or failing drum bearings are typical. We replace worn components before they cause further damage.

Dryer Not Starting

No response when the start button is pressed. Door switch, start switch, motor, or thermal fuse failures cause this. We test the full electrical circuit and replace the faulty component.

Dryer Overheating

Drum excessively hot or the unit shutting off mid-cycle. A blocked exhaust duct or failed high-limit thermostat causes overheating. This is a fire risk and should be addressed promptly.

What are the most common dryer problems in East York homes?

Thermal fuse failure is the most frequent dryer call in East York, driven by long basement vent runs in post-war bungalows. When a vent run of 15-20 feet builds up lint at the elbows, exhaust restriction causes the dryer drum temperature to spike and the thermal fuse trips as a safety measure. The dryer tumbles but produces no heat. Replacing the fuse alone costs $80-$120, but without duct cleaning the fuse will fail again within 3-6 months. We clear the entire duct at every East York service call. The second most common problem is gas igniter wear on older GE and Kenmore gas dryers: igniters last 7-10 years and then fail quietly. Drum belt failure is the third most frequent repair — the belts on older GE and Kenmore units use a wider D-shaped belt that is durable but eventually cracks or slips off the drum when the tensioner spring weakens.
