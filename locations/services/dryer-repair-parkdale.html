Common Dryer Problems We Fix in Parkdale

Dryer Not Heating

The most common dryer complaint. Electric dryers usually have a failed heating element or blown thermal fuse. Gas dryers typically have a bad igniter or defective gas valve coils. We carry heating elements and igniters for the most common dryer models and fix no-heat issues in one visit.

Dryer Takes Multiple Cycles to Dry

Clothes still damp after a full cycle usually indicates a blocked lint filter, clogged exhaust duct, or failing heating element. In Parkdale's older buildings, long or improperly routed vent ducts are a common culprit. We clean the full duct path and restore proper airflow.

Dryer Won't Start

A dryer that clicks but doesn't start, or shows no response at all, usually has a failed door switch, blown thermal fuse, or faulty start switch. We test all safety circuits and control components systematically to identify the failed part and replace it.

Drum Not Turning

A dryer that runs but the drum doesn't spin has a broken drum belt — the most common mechanical failure in all dryer brands. We carry drum belts for Whirlpool, LG, Samsung, GE, Kenmore, and other brands. Belt replacement is typically completed within 45 minutes.

Loud Squealing or Thumping

Squealing from a dryer usually indicates worn drum support rollers or a worn drum glide. Thumping sounds point to a failing idler pulley or items caught in the drum. We disassemble the drum, inspect all bearings and rollers, and replace anything worn before it causes secondary damage.

Dryer Overheating and Shutting Off

A dryer that overheats and trips its thermal cutoff is signalling a blocked vent or failing cycling thermostat. This is a fire-safety issue — the dryer is protecting itself from runaway heat. We clear blockages, replace the cutoff and thermostat, and test exhaust temperature before finishing.
