Gas burner clicking but not lighting, or electric coil not heating. Clogged igniter ports, failed igniter switch, or burnt-out burner element are common causes. We clean ports and replace igniters.

Burner Heating Unevenly

Hot spots or cool zones on the cooking surface. Partially clogged gas ports or a failing electric element cause uneven heating. We clean burner systems and replace elements as needed.

Stove Control Knob Broken

Knob loose, cracked, or not controlling the burner properly. We source manufacturer-matched replacement knobs and repair the underlying valve or switch if needed.

What are the most common stove problems in Greektown homes?

Greektown on the Danforth is defined by semi-detached homes with gas ranges — Greek cooking culture, with its olive oil-intensive cuisine, means gas is overwhelmingly preferred. This creates a very specific service pattern. Burner port clogging is the top call from this neighbourhood: olive oil residue and food particles accumulate in the small ports around the burner head over years of cooking, blocking gas flow and producing weak, uneven, or yellow flames instead of a clean blue ring. Igniter electrode wear is closely behind: the electrode erodes over years of daily use, especially when exposed to cooking residue, and the burner starts clicking repeatedly without lighting. Spark module failure occurs when the igniter module itself fails — typically two or more burners stop sparking at the same time. Gas valve solenoid issues appear in older models where the internal valve mechanism wears and the burner either cannot turn on or cannot fully shut off.
