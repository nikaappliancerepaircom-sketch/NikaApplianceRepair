Common Washer Problems We Fix in Richmond Hill

From leaking to not spinning, our Richmond Hill technicians resolve most washing machine faults in a single visit.

Washer Won't Drain

Water left in the drum after the cycle ends. A clogged pump filter, failed drain pump, or blocked hose are the most common causes. Richmond Hill's moderately hard water can cause mineral buildup in pump filters over time. Fixed same-day for $150-$280.

Won't Spin or Agitate

Clothes come out soaking wet. In large-capacity machines common in Richmond Hill family homes, a worn drive belt, broken coupling, or failing motor bearing under heavy load are typical causes. We test and replace components on-site.

Leaking Water

Water on the laundry room floor is a serious concern in Richmond Hill homes where finished basements are common. Door boot seals on front-loaders, cracked hoses, and pump seals are usual suspects. We identify and fix leaks same-day.

Excessive Vibration and Noise

Heavy thumping or vibration during the spin cycle. In Richmond Hill's larger detached homes, worn drum bearings and shock absorbers under high-load use are common. We restore quiet, balanced operation.

Won't Start or No Power

Nothing happens when you press start. Faulty lid switch, door latch failure, failed timer, or control board issue. We test all electrical components and replace only what has failed.

Not Filling with Water

The washer starts its cycle but barely fills. A clogged or failed water inlet valve is the most common cause — mineral scale from York Region's moderately hard water frequently narrows the valve screens. We replace valves and clean screens to restore full fill.

What should I prepare before the washer technician arrives in Richmond Hill?

Before your Nika technician arrives at your Richmond Hill home, a few quick preparations will speed up the repair. Empty the washer drum completely and place a towel on the floor in front of the machine. Write down all error codes you have seen on the Samsung or LG display -- dE, UE, OE, and LE are the most common in Richmond Hill -- and note whether the problem occurs at a specific point in the cycle (spin, drain, fill, or door lock).
