How does Mississauga water quality affect my appliances? +

Mississauga water quality causes specific appliance problems due to Peel Region's moderately hard water supply:

Peel Region water hardness: 150-180 mg/L dissolved minerals (calcium, magnesium) throughout Mississauga. This is classified as "moderately hard" water and comes from Lake Ontario treatment plants serving all Mississauga neighborhoods.

Dishwashers most affected: White film on glassware despite rinse aid, heating element covered in limescale (reduces heating efficiency), spray arm holes clog with mineral deposits (reduces cleaning power), drain pump damaged by mineral chunks, dishes come out spotted or cloudy.

Washing machines second most affected: Inlet valve screens clog completely (machine won't fill or fills slowly), mineral buildup in hoses reduces water flow, soap scum on clothes from minerals binding with detergent, valve calcification causes leaks.

Refrigerator ice makers third most affected: Cloudy or white ice cubes (mineral deposits), slow ice production (clogged water line), ice maker stops working completely (valve blocked), mineral buildup visible in ice bin.

Our Peel Region water solutions: Professional-grade descaling (stronger than retail products), complete system flush of dishwashers and washing machines, replace clogged inlet valves and screens, clear ice maker lines and install inline filters, clean or replace mineral-damaged heating elements.

Prevention for Mississauga residents: Install whole-house water softener (reduces hardness to <60 mg/L - saves $200-400 yearly in repairs), use rinse aid in dishwashers (reduces deposits by 70%), run monthly descaling cycles with vinegar or citric acid, install inline filters for refrigerator ice makers.

Maintenance schedule: Descale dishwasher every 12-18 months, clean washing machine inlet screens every 6 months, replace ice maker filter every 6 months. Prevents 80% of hard-water failures.
