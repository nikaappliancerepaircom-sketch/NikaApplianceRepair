Dryer Not Heating

Clothes stay damp even after long cycles because the dryer won't heat. This is usually a blown thermal fuse, faulty heating element, bad thermostat, or tripped circuit breaker. For gas dryers, the gas valve or igniter may have failed. We test all heating components, replace faulty parts, and check venting. Most no-heat problems are fixed same-day.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Won't Start or No Power

Pressing start does nothing or the control panel is completely dead. This could be a faulty door switch, blown thermal fuse, bad start switch, or power supply issue. We test all electrical components, check the door latch mechanism, and replace failed parts. Most power issues are diagnosed and fixed same-visit with OEM parts.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Drum Not Turning

Dryer runs but the drum won't spin, so clothes don't tumble. This indicates a broken drive belt, seized drum rollers, faulty motor, or bad idler pulley. We inspect the belt and drum assembly, replace worn components, and test motor function. Drum rotation issues are typically resolved within an hour.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Takes Too Long to Dry

Clothes require multiple cycles to dry completely and energy bills are climbing. Common causes include clogged lint screen, blocked venting, weak heating element, or faulty thermostat. We clean the venting system, test heating efficiency, and replace weak components. Drying performance is restored quickly.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Loud Noises or Squealing

Grinding, thumping, or high-pitched squealing sounds during operation. Worn drum rollers, seized drum bearings, frayed belt, or foreign objects stuck in the blower are typical causes. We replace worn bearings and rollers, install new belts, and remove obstructions. Your dryer will run smoothly and quietly again.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Overheating or Shutting Off

Dryer gets extremely hot and shuts off mid-cycle or won't restart until it cools down. This is usually a faulty high-limit thermostat, clogged vent restricting airflow, or failed cycling thermostat. We clean venting, replace thermostats, and check for proper airflow. Overheating issues are resolved to prevent fire hazards.
